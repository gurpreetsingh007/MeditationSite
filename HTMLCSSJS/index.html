Five Techniques for Beginners

Start with one technique. Keep it simple—2 to 5 minutes is enough. Consistency is the key.

Mindfulness

Observe your breath and sensations—gently returning attention whenever it wanders.

How to practice
  1. Sit comfortably. Soften your gaze or close your eyes.
  2. Notice your breath at the nostrils or belly.
  3. When thoughts arise, acknowledge and return to breath.
  4. End by noticing how you feel—no judgment.

Benefits: clarity, focus, emotional balance.

Loving‑Kindness (Metta)

Silently offer friendly wishes to yourself and others.

How to practice
  1. Repeat phrases like: “May I be safe. May I be well.”
  2. Extend to a friend, a neutral person, and all beings.
  3. Let the tone be gentle; no need to force feelings.

Benefits: warmth, connection, reduced self‑criticism.

Body Scan

Travel attention from head to toe, noticing sensations and relaxing gently.

How to practice
  1. Start at the crown; move through face, shoulders, chest.
  2. Continue arms, hands, torso, hips, legs, and feet.
  3. Note tension and allow it to soften with exhale.

Benefits: relaxation, body awareness, better sleep.

Breath Awareness

Gently count the breath or feel the rhythm—no need to change it.

How to practice
  1. Count exhale 1–10; then start again at 1.
  2. If you lose count, simply restart—no problem.

Benefits: calmer mood, steadier attention.

Mantra

Quietly repeat a soothing word or phrase to steady the mind.

How to practice
  1. Choose a simple word, e.g., “So‑Hum” or “Peace.”
  2. Repeat in sync with the breath—inhale, exhale.

Benefits: focus, easing of rumination.

6‑Week Self‑Reflective Meditation Series

By BayG (Meditation Instructor for Beginners)

Each week builds gently on the last. Explore the theme, try the short practice, and reflect using the prompt provided.

Week 1

Breath Awareness & Centering

Theme: Returning to Self

Practice: Mindful breathing, noticing thoughts without judgment.

Reflection Prompt

“What does calm feel like in my body?”

Takeaway: Breath is the doorway to self‑awareness.

Week 2

Body Scan & Inner Listening

Theme: What Is My Body Telling Me?

Practice: Guided body scan, noticing sensations and stored tension.

Reflection Prompt

“Where do I hold stress, and what might that say about me?”

Takeaway: The body is a mirror for inner emotions.

Week 3

Guided Visualization & Personal Symbols

Theme: Exploring My Inner Landscape

Practice: Visualization of a safe or peaceful place.

Reflection Prompt

“What did I see/feel in my inner sanctuary, and what does it reveal about my needs right now?”

Takeaway: Imagination reveals subconscious truths.

Week 4

Loving‑Kindness & Self‑Compassion

Theme: Learning to Be Kind to Myself

Practice: Sending compassion to self first, then outward.

Reflection Prompt

“What words of kindness do I most need to hear from myself?”

Takeaway: Healing starts with self‑love.

Week 5

Mindfulness of Thoughts & Emotions

Theme: Meeting My Inner World

Practice: Observing thoughts/emotions without attaching to them.

Reflection Prompt

“What recurring thoughts or emotions showed up, and what do they teach me?”

Takeaway: We are not our thoughts; we can observe them with clarity.

Week 6

Walking Meditation & Life Integration

Theme: Carrying Presence Into My Life

Practice: Slow, mindful walking; feeling each step as a metaphor for daily living.

Reflection Prompt

“How can I bring mindfulness and self‑awareness into everyday routines?”

Takeaway: Meditation isn’t separate from life—it is life.

A Gentle 1‑Minute Practice

Tap start and follow the cues. We’ll use “Box Breathing” (4‑4‑4‑4): inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4.

  • Sit upright yet relaxed. Shoulders soft.
  • It’s okay if the mind wanders—just begin again.
  • Stop if you feel dizzy; breathe naturally.
Why meditation?

Why meditate?

Meditation gently trains attention and kindness. Short, regular practice is linked to improved mood, sleep, and focus. Start small, be patient, and let the benefits accumulate.
